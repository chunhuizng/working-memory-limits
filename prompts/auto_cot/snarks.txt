Q: Which statement is sarcastic?
Options:
(A) We all know diamonds are worthless
(B) We all know diamonds are valuable
A: Let's think step by step.
To determine which statement is sarcastic, we need to understand the definition of sarcasm. Sarcasm is a form of irony where the speaker says the opposite of what they actually mean, often to convey humor or criticism.

Looking at the options:
(A) "We all know diamonds are worthless" - This statement is not sarcastic. It is a straightforward statement suggesting that diamonds have no value.
(B) "We all know diamonds are valuable" - This statement is sarcastic. It is saying the opposite of what is commonly believed, implying that diamonds are not actually valuable.
So the answer is (B).

Q: Which statement is sarcastic?
Options:

A: Let's think step by step.
